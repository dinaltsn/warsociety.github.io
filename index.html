01 — Philosophy

Think in constraints,
not preferences.

War Society is a structured geopolitical war game club. Each session presents a real-world scenario — a border standoff, an energy crisis, a military escalation — and assigns members as state actors pursuing incompatible win conditions.

No opinions. No moralising. Every move must be justified with a structural reason: geographic, economic, or commitment-based. The founding question is always the same — what does the map force this actor to do?

Sessions run 90 minutes. Three acts. One economic autopsy at the close. By the end of session one, you will instinctively cost every decision before you finish hearing it.

"The reason war games work intellectually is that they force you to think in constraints, not preferences. That is Clausewitz plus Marshall combined — the entire philosophy in two sentences."

Act I

Set the Terrain

15 minutes

Scenario read aloud. Each actor states their one geographic constraint. No discussion — facts only. The map before the moves.

Act II

The Game

50 minutes — 3 rounds

Each actor makes one move per round. A friction card drawn after round two. No opinions — every move requires structural justification.

Act III

Economic Autopsy

20 minutes

Trace every decision to its economic consequence. Map the feedback loop on the board. Identify who actually won — rarely the actor who fired first.
